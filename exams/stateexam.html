Overview

State Government Exams are conducted by individual states for various positions. Each state has its own Public Service Commission that oversees the recruitment process.

State Public Service Commissions (SPSC)

SPSC conducts exams for state administrative services, including various administrative and revenue services. Candidates must prepare for general studies, aptitude, and state-specific subjects.

    Syllabus: General Studies, Aptitude, and State-specific subjects.
  • Eligibility Criteria: Graduation from a recognized university.
  • Pay Scale: INR 15,600 - 39,100 per month.
  • Job Grade: Class A and B.

State Police Recruitment Exams

These exams are for various police positions, including constables and sub-inspectors. Physical fitness and written tests are crucial components of the selection process.

  • Syllabus: General Knowledge, Reasoning, and Physical Fitness.
  • Eligibility Criteria: 10th/12th depending on the post.
  • Pay Scale: INR 21,700 - 69,100 per month.
  • Job Grade: Group C.

State Teacher Eligibility Tests (TET)

TET is conducted to ensure that candidates meet the required standards for teaching positions in state schools. The exam assesses knowledge of pedagogy, child development, and subject-specific content.

  • Syllabus: Child Development, Pedagogy, and Subject-specific content.
  • Eligibility Criteria: Graduation with a B.Ed. degree.
  • Pay Scale: INR 30,000 - 1,00,000 per month.
  • Job Grade: Teaching positions in state schools.
