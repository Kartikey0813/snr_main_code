Overview

Teaching exams are conducted for recruitment in schools and educational institutions. These exams ensure that candidates meet the required standards for teaching positions.

CTET (Central Teacher Eligibility Test)

The CTET is for candidates aspiring to become teachers in central government schools. It assesses knowledge of pedagogy, child development, and subject-specific content.

  • Syllabus: Child Development, Pedagogy, and Subject-specific content.
  • Eligibility Criteria: Graduation with a B.Ed. degree.
  • Pay Scale: INR 30,000 - 1,00,000 per month.
  • Job Grade: Teaching positions in central schools.

State TETs

Each state conducts its own Teacher Eligibility Test for recruitment of teachers in state government schools. Candidates must prepare for various subjects and teaching methodologies.

  • Syllabus: Child Development, Pedagogy, and Subject-specific content.
  • Eligibility Criteria: Graduation with a B.Ed. degree.
  • Pay Scale: INR 30,000 - 1,00,000 per month.
  • Job Grade: Teaching positions in state schools.

NET (National Eligibility Test)

The NET is for eligibility for assistant professor roles and for Junior Research Fellowship in Indian universities. It tests candidates on research methodology, teaching aptitude, and subject-specific knowledge.

  • Syllabus: Research methodology, Teaching aptitude, and Subject-specific content.
  • Eligibility Criteria: Post-graduation in relevant subjects.
  • Pay Scale: INR 30,000 - 1,00,000 per month (varies by institution).
  • Job Grade: Assistant Professor.
