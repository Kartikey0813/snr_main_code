Central Government Exams

Central government exams are conducted by various agencies for recruitment into different central services. Key exams include:

  • UPSC Civil Services Exam: For recruitment into IAS, IPS, and other central services. The exam consists of a preliminary exam, a main exam, and an interview.
  • SSC Combined Graduate Level Exam: For various posts in ministries and departments. It includes a tiered examination process.
  • RRB NTPC and Group D Exams: For recruitment in Indian Railways. These exams assess candidates for various non-technical and technical positions.

State Government Exams

Each state conducts its own exams for various positions. Key exams include:

  • State Public Service Commissions (SPSC): For state administrative services, including various administrative and revenue services.
  • State Police Recruitment Exams: For various police positions, including constables and sub-inspectors.
  • State Teacher Eligibility Tests (TET): For teaching positions in state schools, ensuring that candidates meet the required standards.

Banking and Insurance Exams

These exams are conducted for recruitment in public sector banks and insurance companies. Key exams include:

  • IBPS PO and Clerk Exams: For recruitment of Probationary Officers and Clerks in public sector banks.
  • SBI PO and Clerk Exams: Conducted by the State Bank of India for various positions.
  • Insurance Exams: For various positions in public and private insurance companies.

Defence Exams

Defence exams are conducted for recruitment into the armed forces. Key exams include:

  • NDA (National Defence Academy): For recruitment into the Army, Navy, and Air Force after 12th grade. The exam includes a written test followed by an SSB interview.
  • CDS (Combined Defence Services): For graduates seeking to join the Indian Military Academy, Indian Naval Academy, and Indian Air Force Academy. It consists of a written exam and an SSB interview.
  • Indian Coast Guard Exams: For recruitment into the Indian Coast Guard for various technical and non-technical positions.

Teaching Exams

Teaching exams are conducted for recruitment in schools and educational institutions. Key exams include:

  • CTET (Central Teacher Eligibility Test): For candidates aspiring to become teachers in central government schools.
  • State TETs: Conducted by individual states for recruitment of teachers in state government schools.
  • NET (National Eligibility Test): For eligibility for assistant professor roles and for Junior Research Fellowship in Indian universities.
